Abstract

This paper presents a neural architecture MVDiffusion++ for sparse-view 3D object reconstruction that synthesizes dense and high-resolution views of an object given one or a few images without camera poses as a condition. Two simple ideas are at the heart of MVDiffusion++: 1) A "pose-free architecture" where standard self-attention among 2D latent features learns 3D consistency across conditional and generation views without explicitly using camera pose information; and 2) A view-level uniform dropout that drops a consistent set of views across layers, enabling us to reduce the training-time memory footprint and achieve dense and high-resolution view synthesis. We use Objaverse dataset for training and Google scanned object dataset for evaluation with standard novel view synthesis and 3D reconstruction metrics, where MVDiffusion++ makes a significant boost over the current state-of-the-art.
